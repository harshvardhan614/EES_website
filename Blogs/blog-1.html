Driver Override System

Author: Jaideep Gupta

With the advancement in technology, the driving has become more and more comfortable. But comfortable driving also raises the need for advanced safety of passengers. Safety features like Anti-lock braking system, crash avoidance systems, automatic parking control, lane keep assist etc. ensure passenger safety. One such feature is driver override system or brake override system and it is one of the most important safety features of a vehicle.

What is it?

Suppose while driving the driver accidentally presses the brake and accelerator pedal simultaneously. One can’t imagine what could happen (and it is not even advised to try this). The Brake override (also known as smart pedal) system’s technology recognizes these mixed signals or electric malfunction. It overrules the command and sends signals to the central computer system to stop the vehicle safely.

How does it work?

Brake override is a fully integrated electronic system. The braking system enables various sensors fixed to the vehicles and transmits signals to the electronic control unit (ECU). This ECU is responsible for checking the position of accelerator and brake pedal and detect any abnormality. If any abnormality is detected, then ECU either reduces the effect of accelerator or overlooks it. In some systems, the ECU overrules the acceleration command partially by stopping the throttle, so the driver won’t be able to accelerate the vehicle. In some systems it minimizes engine’s fuel supply; while in hybrid vehicles, the electronic current force is reduced.

When does it works?

Sometimes accidentally the vehicle accelerates beyond the driver’s intention which it detects through its sensors as an involuntary or accidental acceleration. It might happen when –

There is an electric malfunction and vehicle is over accelerating.

Brake and accelerator pedal are pressed simultaneously.

Sensors detect any object or other vehicle very close to the vehicle and still the car is accelerating.
